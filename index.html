Concept Map

Key Concepts at a Glance

Acid

A proton (H+) donor. Brønsted-Lowry definition. Strong acids fully dissociate (HCl -> H+ + Cl-). Weak acids partially dissociate.

Base

A proton (H+) acceptor. Strong bases (NaOH) fully dissociate. Weak bases accept protons partially (NH3 + H2O <-> NH4+ + OH-).

pH

pH = -log[H+]. Pure water at 25 C has pH = 7. Acids: pH < 7. Bases: pH > 7. Each unit is a 10x change in [H+].

Ka & pKa

Ka is the acid dissociation constant. pKa = -log(Ka). Lower pKa = stronger acid. At the half-equivalence point, pH = pKa.

Why Buffers Work

A buffer contains a weak acid (HA) and its conjugate base (A-) in significant amounts. When you add strong acid (H+), the base A- absorbs it:

A- + H+ -> HA

When you add strong base (OH-), the weak acid donates a proton:

HA + OH- -> A- + H2O

The buffer works best within 1 pH unit of the pKa.
